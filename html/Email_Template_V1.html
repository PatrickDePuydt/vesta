The Missing Field Manual

Field Notes No. 41

New name?
You probably noticed I took the hilariously brazen step of renaming your baby. Hear me out?

( puts on mansplaining jacket ) I think what you're trying to do with this brand is give women a sense of autonomy, and therefore freedom, to make the choices they want without feeling like they have to rely on anyone.

You want to empower people to get out of toxic relationships, jobs, mentalities, situations. It's wilderness survival, for the modern world.

In essence, you want to give women the power to define what home means to them, regardless of where they are in their lives.

Vesta is the Roman Goddess of the "Hearth", which I think means "home", and the Vestal Virgins were focused on maintaining a flame for the good of Rome, ignoring all fleshly desires.

I think there is strength in this comparison, not because we're advocating for celibacy, but competency because dependency can take many forms.

To me, the Backback name ran the risk of not making the right first impression: women carry things in all sorts of ways, seen and unseen.

Ready to Rock!
From the beginning, I designed the brand in Mailchimp as much as I could for 2 reasons.

1. Brands are notoriously soluble when being built In HTML. It's really easy to screw up on a good day, and if you know HTML/CSS, it's still a full-time job.

2. Starting in Mailchimp as my design tool, I hoped the design would look less a clunky representation of the brand. I'm pretty happy with the way it came out.

Font Explanation
I chose fonts that were web-ready, so you didn't have to get the air let out of your tank when we realized the fancy fonts I chose can't be used in an email. Been there, done that. Womp womp.

Icon Explanation
The icons represent the paths we take, sometimes how they intersect, sometimes they converge.

Your bones don't break, mine do. That's clear. Your cells react to bacteria and viruses differently than mine. You don't get sick, I do.

That's also clear.

But for some reason, you and I react the exact same way to water. We swallow it too fast, we choke. We get some in our lungs, we drown. However unreal it may seem, we are connected, you and I. We're on the same curve, just on opposite ends.

Built to grow.
So many brands run-aground when they don't plan for expansion and growth; I like to begin with the end in mind.

What could Vesta be? When you rack up hundreds of editions, what then?

I saw the newsletter being consolidated into an actual book - the Vesta Field Manual.
 

I also wanted to see what the brand would look like as a fake Instagram account.
