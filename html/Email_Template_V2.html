Field Notes No. 41

Backpack is now Vesta!
You probably noticed I took the hilariously brazen step of renaming your baby. Hear me out?

Backpack is about not feeling beholden. Not in debt. Not at anyone's mercy.

Vesta takes that a step further by being about knowing you have the tools to be ok.

( puts on mansplaining sport coat ) This brand is about amplifying a sense of autonomy; to make the choices we want without feeling like they have to rely on anyone.

You want to empower people to get out of toxic relationships, jobs, mentalities, situations. It's wilderness survival, for the modern world.
 

Vesta (Latin pronunciation: [ˈwɛsta]) is the virgin goddess of the hearth, home, and family in Roman religion. She was rarely depicted in human form, and was often represented by the fire of her temple in the Forum Romanum.
- Wikipedia
 
I'm hoping with Vesta, we can further develop the sense of power to define what home means to your readers, regardless of where they are in their lives.

Not all women carry backpacks. Not all women carry purses. Not all women like to carry anything.

Further, I'm reminded of the book "The Things they Carried", where some of the heaviest things were mental.

"Backpack" seemed both too literal (purse users) and too abstract (what if it can't fit in a bag?), while also running the risk of being too narrow (and possibly outgrowing it) but also possibly not making the right first impression.

Ready to Rock!
From the beginning, I designed the brand in Mailchimp as much as I could for 2 reasons.

1. Brands are notoriously soluble when being built In HTML. It's really easy to screw up on a good day, and if you know HTML/CSS, it's still a full-time job.

2. Starting in Mailchimp as my design tool, I hoped the design would look less a clunky representation of the brand. I'm pretty happy with the way it came out.

What could Vesta be? When you rack up hundreds of editions, what then?
